### Key 3.5-level choices I made

- **Made the card PRINTABLE, not just readable.** The card is designed for card stock, lamination, and folding. **The 50+ player who prints it and carries it has the system at hand.**
- **Made the card the CONSOLIDATION of 4 deep dives, not a new deep dive.** The card is the QUICK REFERENCE; the deep dives are the FULL REFERENCE. **Print the card, read the deep dives at home.**
- **Made the 17 sections fit on 1 page.** Each section is 1-3 lines, dense but readable. The bilingual version is denser; the English/Vietnamese expanded versions are larger for readability.
- **Added the "How to Use" guide** — before, during, and after the match. **The 50+ player knows exactly when to glance at the card (before serve, between points, at changeover).**
- **Added the 3 sound cues** (Bộp, Bịch, Phập) as a unique feature. **The sound cues are the only audio reference on the card — useful for self-diagnosis without looking.**
- **Added the 4 protected joints** to remind the 50+ player of the WHY (joint protection, not just the HOW).
- **Added the 4 numbers** to show the cost of fumbling (6-7.5 min/match) and the benefit of the map (20% win rate impact).
- **Phrased the longevity framing:** *"20+ years of tennis starts with 1 card. The 50+ player with the card plays tennis. The 50+ player without plays 'search for the grip.'"*
