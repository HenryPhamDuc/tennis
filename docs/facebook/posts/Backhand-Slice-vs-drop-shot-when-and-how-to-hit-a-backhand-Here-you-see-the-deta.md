Backhand Slice vs drop shot, when and how to hit a backhand? Here you see the details from the top coaches.

The most essential part of the one-handed backhand is spacing to a clean contact point in front of the body.

Everything else depends on that:

a full shoulder turn

a stable head

a long, relaxed hitting arm

contact out in front with the body rotating through

One-handed backhands fail most often when the player is too close, too late, or reaching with the arm instead of moving the feet and body into position.

A simple cue: turn early, set the spacing, then drive through contact in front.
